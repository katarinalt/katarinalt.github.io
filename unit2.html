Which to websites are you most inspired by?

  • SATURDAYS NYC

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size or balance?

  • For Saturdays NYC the top slide show works as a focal point for the viewer as it covers the entire top section. As we move throughout the website they have achieved great hierarchy flow by using different sized imagery to convey different segments of the website, as well as having chosen a white negative space the gently puts each element into a grid system.
  • Stutterheim has chosen a white negative space that makes the companyâ€™s logo and name the focal point as you enter the website. The reader is meant to see the headline and subheadline before moving the eyes down towards the slide show of product images. Stutterheim achieves great visual hierarchy in its presentation, and by highlighting the different sections in various image and text sizes the flow of the website is easy to follow.

What makes them similar and different?

  • They share similarities in the way the use imagery to highlight their products, by first have a slide show filling out the top section of their website, to dividing each next section into different images. What is different is where they have place the focal point; Saturdays NYC wants the viewer to look at the product first and then look around the website, whereas Stutterheim has placed its focal point on its logo making the viewer remember the name before browsing the products.What I like about Saturdays NYC is that the top header stays as you scroll down the website allowing the viewer to always click on either shop, locations, home etc. if he needs to. I also really enjoy the simplicity of using white negative space and very little text in between the images allowing the product to speak for themselves.
